A closer look.

54 is the macOS keycode for the right ⌘ key — rarely used and mostly idle, making it the perfect key to modify, and a logical name for this app.

One key, one app

Hold the right ⌘ key — no chord, no clicks, no palette to search. Pure muscle memory.

Out and back

The same hold that summons your app sends you back — even if it wasn't running yet.

Calm by design

A short, deliberate charge before it fires, so switching never feels frantic.

Keeps your shortcuts

Quick taps and normal right-⌘ shortcuts pass straight through — only a deliberate hold fires.

Tiny & invisible

No Dock icon, no menu bar clutter. Starts at login and stays out of the way.

Easier on everyone

One forgiving key hold is genuinely simpler — a real accessibility win.

Pick the one app you're always dropping into and back out of. The pattern is always the same: summon, do the thing, dismiss.

Browser

Need to look something up mid-sentence? All your browsing sessions come forward exactly as you left them — no new window, no search box.

Email

Glance at a message and reply, then drop straight back into focus — without getting sucked in and losing the thread of deeper work.

Notes

A thought worth capturing never means hunting for the right window — jot it down while it's still fresh.

Terminal

Run a quick install or git command mid-task, check a deploy. The full terminal with every tab and session, not a stripped-down drawer.

Assistant

Bind Claude or your AI chatbot of choice — ask a question or paste a snippet, and come back with the answer.

Shortcut

Save any Shortcut you've built as an app (File → Add to Dock) and bind it — mute your mic, start a timer, run whatever you've automated.

Key54 gives a single dedicated key to the app you most often switch in and out of mid-task. It's made to work alongside your favorite launcher, not replace it.
